Our story

About LandMarque

LandMarque exists to connect people with the private estates, historic houses, and working landscapes that define the English countryside — and to help the families and organisations that steward these places sustain them for future generations.

Many of the most remarkable places in England are privately owned: ancient deer parks, chalk-stream valleys, working farms, woodland gardens, and Georgian houses that never appear on a tourist map. These places are often open to the public in some form, but finding them, understanding them, and contributing to their upkeep has always been difficult.

We built LandMarque to change that. For visitors, it is a guide to the estates and landscapes around them — their history, their walking routes, their places to eat, and the people who look after them. For estate owners and managers, it is a platform that turns every visitor into a potential supporter: handling parking, donations, bench sponsorships, and legacy gifts in a way that respects the character of the estate and requires almost no effort to set up.

What we believe

Stewardship first
The estates in our network are living landscapes, not tourist attractions. We design everything around long-term stewardship, not short-term footfall.
Honest presentation
We write about every estate with care and accuracy. We do not publish content we haven't verified, and we update it when things change.
Light-touch technology
A land agent should be able to set up a LandMarque page in an afternoon. We do not add complexity where simplicity serves.
Direct benefit
Every donation, parking fee, and bench sponsorship goes directly to the estate. LandMarque takes a small platform fee — nothing more.

LandMarque is based in the Surrey Hills. If you manage an estate and would like to discuss joining the network, we would be glad to hear from you.
