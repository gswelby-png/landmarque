Places of Interest

Every estate has things that make it remarkable — ancient trees, ha-has, walled gardens, historic buildings, wildlife hides, viewpoints that have been loved for generations. These things are often invisible to first-time visitors.

We help you surface and share them. Each point of interest is added to your estate page with a photograph, a short description, and — where appropriate — precise location information so visitors can find it on foot. Points of interest can be added and updated through your estate dashboard at any time, and removed seasonally when access restrictions apply.

Highlighting what is genuinely special about your land serves a dual purpose: it draws visitors deeper in, increasing the time they spend and the likelihood that they pay for parking; and it helps to justify, gently and without fanfare, why that contribution matters.

Examples from our estates

  • — Ancient and veteran trees — oaks, yews, and limes with centuries of history
  • — Viewpoints and vistas that reward the walk to reach them
  • — Historic features — walled gardens, ice houses, follies, ha-has
  • — Wildlife habitats — heron rookeries, bat roosts, wildflower meadows
  • — Film and TV locations — places that carry a story beyond the landscape
